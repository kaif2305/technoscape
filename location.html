The VIT campus is located on a 370-acre site on the Ranipet – Katpadi Road. It is close to two prominent metropolitan cities, Chennai and Bengaluru. Both cities are well connected by road, rail, and air to all parts of the country. The cities have prominent international airports, the nearest being in Chennai (166 kms), followed by Bengaluru (209 kms).

Upon Reaching Madras International Meenambakkam Airport (MAA), Chennai

Travel to Chennai Central Railway Station (19 kms) or to the Koyambedu bus stand (16 kms) whereon you can travel by either of the two modes:

By Train

  • Chennai Central Railway Station to Katpadi Junction Railway Station is about 130 kms.
  • Travel time is about 2½ hours.
  • Upon reaching, take a 3 km auto rickshaw ride to VIT campus.

By Bus

  • You can either travel via taxi or bus.
  • Travelling a distance of 130 kms (approx) will take about 2 ½ hours by taxi.
  • Travel time via bus is about 3 hrs from Koyambedu Bus station.

Upon Reaching Kempegowda International Airport, Bengaluru

Travel to KSR Bengaluru City Junction (34 kms) or the Majestic Bus Stand in Gandhi Nagar (34 kms) or the Satellite Bus Stand at Mysore Road (40 kms), from where you can travel by either of the two modes:

By Train

  • KSR Bengaluru City Junction to Katpadi Junction is about 219 kms.
  • Travel time is about 3 hours.
  • Once you reach, take a 3 km auto rickshaw ride to VIT campus.

By Bus

  • You can either travel via taxi or bus.
  • Travelling a distance of 210 kms (approx) will take about 4 hours via taxi.
  • Travel time by bus is about 4 hrs from both Majestic and Satellite bus stands.

Auto rickshaws (on share basis) are easily available from Katpadi Junction Railway station and Vellore new bus stand. Government buses will run from Chittoor bus stop to VIT. You could also hire exclusive auto rickshaws and MAYA TAXI to reach VIT.

  1. Vellore Fort

    2. Vellore Fort is a 16th-century fort in the city of Vellore, Tamil Nadu, India. With a unique blend of Hindu, Islamic, and European architecture, it is known for its impressive structures such as the Jalakandeswarar Temple and St. John's Church. It also has a museum that showcases artifacts from the fort's history, making it a popular tourist destination. With an approximate distance of 10 km (6.2 miles) from the VIT campus, it is a 30 minute ride via car or taxi.

  2. Sripuram Golden Temple

    3. A temple complex in Vellore, Tamil Nadu, dedicated to the Hindu goddess Mahalakshmi, it is known for its impressive golden exterior, intricate carvings, and murals depicting Hindu deities. The temple's main sanctum houses the largest golden statue of the goddess Mahalakshmi in the world. The complex also has a museum displaying artifacts related to the temple's history. With an approximate distance of 18 km (11.2 miles) from the VIT campus, it is a 30 minute ride by car or taxi.

  3. Amirthi Zoological Park

    4. A wildlife sanctuary and zoo located in Vellore district, Tamil Nadu, India, it is known for its natural beauty, scenic waterfalls, and a variety of flora and fauna. The park covers an area of 25 hectares, visitors can explore it through various trails that lead to the waterfalls. The park also has a small zoo with deers, monkeys, peacocks, and birds; also the visitors can enjoy boating in the park's lake. It is a popular tourist destination for those interested in experiencing the natural beauty and wildlife of Tamil Nadu. With an approximate distance of 25 km (15.5 miles) from the VIT campus, it is a 45 minute ride by car or taxi.

  4. Karigiri Hills

    5. A beautiful range of hills in the Vellore district of Tamil Nadu, India, it is known for its scenic beauty, lush greenery, and rock formations, including several million-year-old caves with intricate carvings. The hills are a popular destination for trekking and hiking, where visitors can enjoy beautiful views from the top. It is a serene place to escape the city and enjoy the natural beauty of Tamil Nadu. With an approximate distance of 17 km (10.5 miles) from the VIT campus, it is a 30 minute ride by car or taxi.

  5. Yelagiri Hills

    6. A hill station in the Vellore district of Tamil Nadu, India, it is known for its natural beauty, pleasant climate, and adventure sports. It offers hiking, trekking, and nature walks in the surrounding lush green forests, where visitors can enjoy panoramic views from several viewpoints, including the Swamimalai Hill. Other popular attractions include Punganoor Lake, Jalagamparai Waterfalls, and Government Herbal Farm, which houses several medicinal plants. With an approximate distance of 55 km (34 miles) from the VIT campus, it is a 2 hour ride by car or taxi.

  6. Palar Dam

    7. The Palar Dam is a masonry gravity dam built across the Palar River, and is an important source of water for irrigation and drinking water supply to Vellore and Tiruvannamalai districts. The dam is also a popular tourist spot and offers scenic views of the surrounding hills and water bodies. With an approximate distance of 23 km (14.3 miles) from the VIT campus, it is a 60 minute ride by car or taxi.
